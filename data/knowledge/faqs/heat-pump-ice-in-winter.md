# Heat Pump Ice Build-Up in Winter

## Is ice on the outdoor unit normal?

Light frost on the outdoor coil during defrost cycles is **normal** for air-source heat pumps operating below 40°F ambient. However, **thick ice encasing the unit** is not normal and indicates:

- Restricted airflow (blocked coil, snow drift against unit)
- Low refrigerant charge
- Failed defrost board or sensor
- Stuck reversing valve

## What the customer can do safely

- Clear snow and debris in a **2-foot perimeter** around the outdoor unit
- Do **not** chip ice with tools — coil fins bend easily
- Switch to auxiliary/emergency heat on the thermostat temporarily if indoor temperature drops below 65°F

## Service recommendation

Schedule **P2 urgent** service when ice persists more than 4 hours or indoor temperature falls more than 5°F below setpoint. Document recurrence — third occurrence within 90 days may indicate warranty claim eligibility on qualifying Lennox and Carrier models.

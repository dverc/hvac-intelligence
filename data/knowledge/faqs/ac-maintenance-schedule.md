# AC Maintenance Schedule — Residential Customers

## How often should I service my central AC?

Carrier and Trane units in hot climates (90°F+ summer design) should receive **professional maintenance twice per year**:

1. **Spring tune-up** (March–April): refrigerant check, coil cleaning, capacitor test, thermostat calibration.
2. **Fall inspection** (September–October): heat mode verification for heat pumps, defrost cycle test, filter housing check.

## Warning signs between visits

- Warm air from supply vents when cooling mode is selected
- Ice on the outdoor condenser line set
- Short cycling (unit turns on/off every 2–3 minutes)
- Unusual grinding or squealing from the blower motor

## Filter replacement

Replace 1-inch pleated filters every **60–90 days**. MERV 11–13 is recommended for homes with pets or allergy sufferers. Never run the system without a filter installed.

## Priority dispatch

Customers reporting **no cooling** during heat advisories qualify for **P1 emergency dispatch** when outdoor temperatures exceed 95°F and the home has vulnerable occupants (elderly, infants, medical equipment).

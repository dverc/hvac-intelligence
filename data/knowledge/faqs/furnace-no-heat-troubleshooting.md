# Furnace No-Heat Troubleshooting Guide

## Quick checks before calling for service

1. **Thermostat**: Confirm mode is HEAT, setpoint is above room temperature, and batteries are fresh (if battery-powered).
2. **Power**: Verify the furnace switch (looks like a light switch near the unit) is ON. Check the breaker panel for a tripped breaker labeled HVAC or Furnace.
3. **Filter**: A severely clogged filter can trip high-limit safety switches. Replace if dirty.

## When to schedule a technician

| Symptom | Likely cause | Recommended priority |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|
| Blower runs, no warm air | Ignitor or flame sensor | P2 (next-day) |
| Clicking but no ignition | Failed hot surface ignitor | P2 |
| Burning smell on first start | Dust burn-off (normal once) | Monitor |
| Gas odor | Possible leak — **evacuate and call gas utility** | P1 emergency |

## Warranty note

Units under **annual maintenance contract** receive no diagnostic fee for no-heat calls placed before 10 AM on weekdays.
